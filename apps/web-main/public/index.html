Node One · Hardware

K1-Lightwave: Your First PRISM.node

A dual-core, audio-reactive light bar built for both ambience and experimentation.

K1-Lightwave is a dual edge-lit light bar with 320 addressable RGB LEDs—more than any consumer RGB device on the market. It sits behind your setup and turns sound into fluid, volumetric light. Built around a dual-core ESP32-S3 and a custom light-guide diffusion system, it ships with ambience presets ready to run and firmware open for hacking.

What's in the Founders' Edition hardware:

  • Dual-core ESP32-S3 for parallel audio + render pipelines
  • 320 addressable RGB LEDs across dual edge-lit light-guide panels
  • I²S MEMS microphone for direct sound capture
  • Custom edge-lit diffusion system for volumetric glow
  • USB-C power with brightness management
  • Pre-loaded ambience modes + unlocked firmware access

How It Feels

Physics, Not Pixels.

Fluid light, not flickering noise.

Most RGB lights trigger pixels on and off. K1-Lightwave runs a 125 Hz physics-driven renderer, so kicks, snares, and melodies appear as continuous motion—light flowing through a volume, not a grid stuttering on your wall.

  • 125 Hz render loop for ultra-smooth motion
  • 96-bin frequency resolution for precise audio analysis
  • <8ms audio-to-light sync latency
